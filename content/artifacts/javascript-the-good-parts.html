JavaScript

A surgical extraction of elegance from the chaos of the web.

by Douglas Crockford

This essential guide acts as a scalpel, scraping away JavaScript's dangerous quirks and baffling design flaws to leave you with a streamlined toolkit for building robust software.

Most programming languages contain good and bad parts, but JavaScript has more than its share of the bad, having been developed and released in a hurry before it could be refined.

In JavaScript: The Good Parts, Crockford finally digs through the steaming pile of good intentions and blunders to give you a detailed look at all the genuinely elegant parts of JavaScript.
